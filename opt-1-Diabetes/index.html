Ravi Joshi

Chief Editor - Digital

Ravi Joshi was editing Bangalore Mirror when Covid’s second wave caught him by the scruff of the neck. He was admitted to hospital -- a severe cytokine storm had set in – and administered corticosteroids, among other drugs. He returned from hospital with an HbA1c of 9.8 – a full-blown diabetic with dangerously high levels of blood glucose. He was advised medication and insulin. Determined not to consign his life to drugs, RJ started climbing stairs. With more research, he changed his diet to include more fiber and protein. A Continuous Glucose Monitor (CGM) patch gave him further insights into his body and its fueling/exercise needs. Within four months, his HbA1C had come down to 6.2 and to 5.3 in another 3 months. Currently, he is at 5.7. He has sweets every now and then, delves deep into diabetes research and keeps walking.
